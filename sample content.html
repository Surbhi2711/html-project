To optimize the operation of a datacenter, it is crucial to consider and minimize all energy components including information technology (IT), cooling and network energy consumptions. Consolidation of multiple active VMs onto a single physical server as well as consolidating multiple turned on servers into a single rack are examples of techniques that reduce the energy overheads by decreasing the number of turned on racks and physical servers [14].
VM consolidation is used in virtualized datacenters which typically provide internet-wide services such as mail and social networking. Therefore, it is important to adopt simple and fast dynamic algorithms with low time complexity. This paper proposes four energy-aware resource management algorithms for virtualized datacenters which leads to reduction in total energy consumption of datacenters. With appropriate allocation of VMs to servers, the number of active servers can be decreased. This problem is similar to the bin packing problem [7,10,15]. Bin packing is an NP-hard problem, and hence, approximation algorithms are typically used to solve corresponding optimization problem. Some of the most well-known methods to assign VMs to PMs are First Fit Decreasing, Round Robin and Best Fit Decreasing algorithms.
